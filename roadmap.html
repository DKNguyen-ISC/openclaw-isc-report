Kent's Original Question

"Vậy giá trị sử dụng cho cá nhân, cho từng bộ phận để Kaizen, 5S, 7-wastes, tăng hiệu suất?"

Translation: "What value does it have for individuals and departments to do Kaizen, 5S, reduce 7-wastes, improve efficiency?"

This is exactly the right question. The theory is sound — AI agents that eliminate repetitive tasks would directly address Waiting, Defects, and Over-processing waste. The problem is that OpenClaw, in its current form, creates more work than it saves for typical office users.

Level 1: Individual Employees

⚠️ NOT READY FOR GENERAL STAFF

Why It Doesn't Work Yet

  • ✗ Requires command-line setup — coding knowledge needed
  • ✗ Requires continuous supervision — AI errors compound quickly
  • ✗ When it fails, it fails silently — staff won't know until damage is done
  • ✗ Each person needs their own isolated environment

Who Could Use It TODAY

  • ✓ IT staff or technical engineers comfortable with Linux/Python
  • ✓ Personal, non-sensitive tasks only (document formatting, file organization)
  • ! NOT connected to ISC systems under any circumstances

Level 2: Department Automation

❌ NOT SUITABLE FOR DEPARTMENTAL AUTOMATION

DK's data automation approach already does what OpenClaw promises, but reliably. The SCM shortage calculation is the clearest example:

OLD WAY (Manual)
  • Open 4 Excel files separately
  • Cross-reference demand vs. stock manually
  • Calculate shortage by hand
  • Email Purchasing with findings
  • Time: 10 hours/week
VS
NEW WAY (DK's Automation)
  • BigQuery SQL runs: SELECT Demand - Stock AS Shortage
  • Apps Script emails Purchasing at 06:00 automatically
  • Runs while the entire team is still asleep
  • Time: 0 hours/week

OpenClaw's approach to this same task would be:

Watch the screen → open Excel → drag formulas → copy-paste results. Higher chance of formula error, incorrect cell references, skipped rows. Needs someone watching to verify output.

Level 3: Company-Wide Enterprise

❌ NOT APPROPRIATE FOR ENTERPRISE DEPLOYMENT

Enterprise deployment of OpenClaw at ISC's scale requires:
